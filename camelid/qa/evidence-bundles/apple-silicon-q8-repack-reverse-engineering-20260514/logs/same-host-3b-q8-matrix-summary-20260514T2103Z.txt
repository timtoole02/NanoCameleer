Interpretation: single-run 1-token TTFT is noisy and not a speed claim. It still proves the architecture boundary: llama.cpp is much faster on the same CPU-only Q8_0 work because its Q8 tensors are backend-owned CPU_REPACK storage consumed directly by arm int8 kernels; Camelid's legacy sidecar copies are slower/noisier and should stay debug/parity-only. The current runtime-owned Camelid storage is the right direction, but the Mac slice needs broader direct-kernel coverage and warm multi-token measurement before claiming performance parity.

Blocker reproduced during matrix attempt: `CAMELID_MAC_Q8_REPACK=on CAMELID_Q8_0_BLOCK_DOT=off` crashed in `src/tensor/mod.rs:1159` with empty file-backed tensor data. Do not market that as packed-off; packed-off means repack/sidecars disabled while Q8 block-dot remains enabled. This fallback guard should be fixed before expanding the env surface.
